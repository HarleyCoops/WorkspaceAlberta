# WorkspaceAlberta Homepage Brand / Design Spec

Working direction for the next `index.html` redesign.

## Core repositioning

WorkspaceAlberta is no longer just a procurement intelligence workspace.

It is a Canadian hardware-and-platform company for industrial business owners: a dedicated AI terminal, installed in the owner's office, configured around the company's work, and supported as managed infrastructure.

The page should make the product feel physical before it explains the software.

The customer should understand this in the first 10 seconds:

- WorkspaceAlberta is a dedicated AI workstation, not another browser tab.
- The device is installed for the owner, not handed to an IT department.
- The platform is built for concrete, lumber, aluminum, fabrication, construction, and adjacent industrial companies.
- The ideal customer is an owner-led company with roughly 18-50 employees.
- The long-term ambition is Canadian models, Canadian hardware, and eventually Canadian-built Linux devices.
- The near-term product is an off-the-shelf but highly designed terminal built around Raspberry Pi 5, Hermes Agent, Codex-class agentic workflows, Cohere/Canadian model routes, and custom business wiring.

## Positioning sentence

WorkspaceAlberta designs, assembles, and operates dedicated enterprise AI workspaces for Canadian industrial business owners.

## Hero concept

The homepage opens with a full-bleed image or render of the complete WorkspaceAlberta terminal:

- clean Raspberry Pi 5 visible as the hardware core
- white/silver dual monitors
- symmetrical monitor geometry
- central mast or balanced arms
- brushed aluminum base
- white USB-C power and white micro-HDMI cable discipline
- exposed copper/silver cooling detail
- no clutter, no gaming look, no maker-table mess

The image should feel like:

- Bloomberg terminal discipline
- Apple industrial design restraint
- Canadian manufacturing seriousness
- a premium equipment install, not consumer electronics

The Pi can be visible, but it should not look like a hobby board. It should look intentionally mounted inside a designed executive terminal.

## Opening copy

Primary hero tagline:

> Welcome to Work, Alberta.

Supporting line:

> A dedicated AI terminal for the owners building Canada's concrete, lumber, and aluminum economy.

Longer hero paragraph:

> WorkspaceAlberta installs a managed AI workspace inside owner-led industrial companies. It is always on, physically present, and built around the practical question that starts every useful system: Wouldn't it be great if...?

CTA direction:

- Primary: `Build the workspace`
- Secondary: `See the terminal`
- Avoid generic CTAs like `Get Started`, `Learn More`, or `Unlock AI`.

## Brand voice

Plain, direct, industrial, ambitious.

Not SaaS. Not crypto. Not generic AI transformation.

Good phrases:

- dedicated workspace
- owner terminal
- managed AI infrastructure
- Canadian demand, Canadian models, Canadian hardware
- practical intelligence for the companies doing the work
- built for the owner, not the IT backlog
- always on, always in the room
- Wouldn't it be great if...

Avoid:

- unlock your potential
- supercharge productivity
- seamless synergy
- AI-powered transformation
- revolutionize your business
- generic dashboards
- vague automation claims

## Audience

Primary buyer:

Owner/operator or CEO of a Canadian industrial company with approximately 18-50 employees.

Likely sectors:

- concrete
- lumber
- aluminum
- fabrication
- millwork
- construction supply
- industrial contracting
- specialty manufacturing

Audience psychology:

- They do not want a portal to babysit.
- They do not want another SaaS login.
- They want someone to sit beside the business, understand the work, and build useful systems.
- They value practical outcomes over demos.
- They may not care what MCP, agents, model routing, or vector search are, but they care whether the terminal finds demand, saves time, and turns ideas into built systems.

## Design system direction

### Palette

Base:

- warm white / porcelain
- brushed aluminum
- soft concrete gray
- charcoal text
- copper accent from exposed cooling
- Alberta sky blue used sparingly as a signal color

Suggested tokens:

- `--paper: #f7f5ef`
- `--white: #ffffff`
- `--aluminum: #d6d3ca`
- `--concrete: #b9b7ae`
- `--ink: #111111`
- `--muted: #5f625f`
- `--copper: #b87333`
- `--sky: #79a9d8`
- `--line: rgba(17,17,17,0.14)`

### Typography

Use large, confident editorial typography for headlines and precise sans-serif for body/interface labels.

Direction:

- headline: modern grotesk or editorial sans, large and spare
- body: highly readable sans
- technical accents: small mono labels only where useful

Do not make the whole site monospaced. The product is technical, but the brand is executive-industrial.

### Layout

Use fewer, stronger sections.

The page should scroll like a product film:

1. Full-bleed terminal image
2. Tagline roll-up: `Welcome to Work, Alberta.`
3. Short positioning block
4. The terminal as a physical product
5. The platform as a business-building workspace
6. Family-doctor analogy
7. `Wouldn't it be great if...` idea engine
8. Canadian stack / sovereignty path
9. Installation and subscription model
10. Closing call to build the first deployment

The design should be cinematic but not flashy. White space, strong grid, and high-resolution hardware imagery should carry the page.

## Homepage section outline

### 1. Full-bleed terminal hero

Purpose: make the product real.

Visual:

- full viewport image/render
- white symmetrical terminal
- exposed Pi 5 core
- premium desk object

Text treatment:

- minimal overlay or text below image
- `Welcome to Work, Alberta.` appears as a roll-up or reveal beneath the hero image
- motion should feel mechanical/precise, not bouncy

Draft copy:

> Welcome to Work, Alberta.
>
> A dedicated AI terminal for the owners building Canada's concrete, lumber, and aluminum economy.

### 2. What it is

Purpose: define the category.

Draft copy:

> WorkspaceAlberta is a dedicated AI workspace installed inside owner-led industrial companies. We design the terminal, assemble the hardware, configure the agent platform, and build the workflows around the way the company already works.
>
> It is not a chatbot subscription. It is a managed workspace for ideas, tenders, documents, estimates, product development, and operational improvement.

### 3. Built for 18-50 employee companies

Purpose: sharpen ICP.

Draft copy:

> We work with the companies big enough to feel the pain and small enough that the owner still carries the system in their head.
>
> Around 18-50 employees is where the opportunity is clear: enough moving parts to need AI, not enough overhead to survive another platform rollout.

### 4. The family doctor analogy

Purpose: explain high-trust ongoing support.

Concept:

WorkspaceAlberta is to the business what a family doctor is to a household: persistent context, continuity, practical triage, and a long-term relationship.

Draft copy:

> Most AI tools behave like walk-in clinics. You explain the problem again, get a one-off answer, and leave with no continuity.
>
> WorkspaceAlberta is closer to a family doctor for the business. It remembers the company context, tracks recurring friction, knows what systems matter, and helps decide what needs attention now, what can wait, and what should be built next.

Important: do not over-medicalize. This is an analogy about continuity of care, not a healthcare claim.

### 5. Wouldn't it be great if...

Purpose: make the product emotionally useful.

Draft copy:

> Every useful system starts as a sentence the owner already says:
>
> Wouldn't it be great if the estimator saw the right tender before the deadline?
> Wouldn't it be great if the shop knew which quote was stuck?
> Wouldn't it be great if the owner could ask across documents, bids, suppliers, and old decisions from one place?
> Wouldn't it be great if good ideas did not die because nobody had time to wire them together?
>
> WorkspaceAlberta turns those sentences into working tools.

### 6. Hardware and platform

Purpose: bridge physical device and software stack.

Draft copy:

> The terminal is the visible part. The platform behind it is the real business system: Hermes Agent, Codex-class agentic workflows, Canadian model routes where possible, procurement tools, document processing, and custom integrations built around the company.
>
> The device gives the owner a place to work. The platform gives the business a way to compound what it learns.

Hardware copy:

> The first generation is assembled from proven off-the-shelf hardware: Raspberry Pi 5 control node, fast local NVMe storage, white/silver displays, aluminum mounting, and managed remote support. The ten-year path is clear: Canadian-built Linux terminals for Canadian industrial AI.

### 7. Canadian stack / sovereignty path

Purpose: connect business story to national-industrial story.

Draft copy:

> Canadian companies should not discover Canadian demand through foreign defaults forever.
>
> WorkspaceAlberta starts with practical tools: CanadaBuys, Alberta Purchasing Connection, Cohere routes, open agent infrastructure, and local managed terminals. Over time, the direction is Canadian models, Canadian deployment, Canadian support, and Canadian hardware.

### 8. Installation model

Purpose: make the pricing feel like equipment + service.

Draft copy:

> We install one terminal for the owner. We configure it around the business. We support it remotely. We keep improving the workflows.
>
> The setup fee covers deployment, installation, configuration, onboarding, and the first serious buildout. The monthly subscription covers the managed workspace, support, updates, model access, and ongoing system development.

### 9. Closing

Purpose: bring it back to work.

Draft copy:

> This is not AI as a tab. This is AI as a place to work.
>
> Welcome to Work, Alberta.

CTA:

- `Design the first terminal`
- `Talk about the workspace`

## Motion direction

Use motion sparingly.

Recommended:

- hero image fades/slides in with a very slow scale
- tagline rolls up from below after image settles
- section dividers use subtle mechanical wipe or line draw
- `Wouldn't it be great if...` lines appear one at a time
- hardware labels can pin briefly around the Pi/monitor/base image

Avoid:

- parallax gimmicks
- constant floating cards
- random animated numbers
- generic glowing AI orbs
- excessive scroll-jacking

## Image requirements

The page depends on a strong hero image.

Ideal image brief:

- front-on or slight three-quarter view
- dual white/silver 27-inch monitors
- symmetric arms or central mast
- brushed aluminum base
- visible Pi 5 with copper/silver cooling
- white cables routed cleanly
- neutral studio background
- no keyboard clutter unless perfectly staged
- no people in the first hero image

If the final photo/render is not ready, use a disciplined placeholder block labelled:

`WorkspaceAlberta Terminal Image / Render`

Do not use generic stock office photography.

## Information architecture

The page should not lead with procurement tools anymore.

Old hierarchy:

1. Canadian procurement intelligence
2. MCP tools
3. bid briefs
4. technical notes

New hierarchy:

1. dedicated AI terminal
2. industrial owner audience
3. high-touch business-building support
4. hardware/platform stack
5. Canadian sovereignty path
6. procurement intelligence as one important starting workflow

Procurement remains important, but it becomes the first concrete workflow inside a larger hardware/platform story.

## Design risks

1. Raspberry Pi can look too cheap if staged poorly.
   - Solve with aluminum base, cable discipline, premium monitors, copper/silver cooling, and product photography.

2. Bloomberg inspiration can feel derivative.
   - Use the principle of a dedicated executive terminal, not Bloomberg's exact look.

3. Canadian hardware claim could overstate current reality.
   - Say `assembled now` and `Canadian-built Linux devices over the long path`, not `manufactured in Canada` yet.

4. The family doctor analogy could sound strange if overdone.
   - Use it once to explain continuity, context, and triage.

5. The page could become too broad.
   - Keep every section tied to the owner terminal and the 18-50 employee industrial business.

## Implementation notes for `index.html`

When we build:

- create a single polished landing page first
- use semantic HTML sections
- keep CSS variables at the top
- support responsive layouts but optimize desktop first
- include reduced-motion CSS
- use local image path placeholders until final hero asset exists
- avoid external dependencies unless there is a strong reason
- do not add fake stats or fake testimonials
- keep copy sparse and strong
